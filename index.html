Portfolio

Coldwater Ranch: Trust For Public Land

The Advanced Cartographic Design course at the University of Montana requires students to reach out to an organization in the community and create them a map. The project was designed to give me the opportunity to utilize both the theory and the software skills learned in the class. The project fulfills the requirements of a Service Learning Course. I utilized my advanced cartographic techniques in typography, color theory, shaded relief to design and construct a map for the Trust for Public Land. This map was created using ArcMap 10.4, Adobe Illustrator, and Adobe Photoshop.

Bike Route

This project shows soil types (Basco, Elkmound, Council, and Orion) found within Pleasant Valley, Wisconsin based on environmental covariates. To predict soil type, I used slope gradient, elevation, and profile curvature as covariates. SoLIM and ArcMap were used to run the analysis.

2010 Hurricane Tracking Map

The purpose of this map was to utilize the Tracking Analyst feature of ArcGIS. Tracking Analyst allowed me explore the relationship of data along a timeline, create a data clock graphic, and play an animated progression of events.

Outside Magazine's Top 25 Cities 2017

Outside Magazine publishes a list of 25 cities and towns every year that the editors believe to be idealic for their subscribers. This interactive web map explores the population change over 10 years for the 2017 TOp 25 Cities. This map was created using Leaflet.js.
